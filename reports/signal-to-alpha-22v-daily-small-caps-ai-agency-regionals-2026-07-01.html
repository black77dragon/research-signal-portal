H. Appendix

Small caps work when breadth, earnings, and valuation line up.

The small-cap thesis is not simply that small companies are cheap. Cheap assets can stay cheap. The better version is that small caps are cheap while earnings expectations are improving and market leadership is broadening. That gives the idea a mechanism.

Jordi's AI framework is valuable because it turns AI investing into an operating process.

Signal means ignoring headlines unless earnings, adoption, and price action confirm them. Alpha means focusing on the companies where the evidence is strongest. Agency means using AI personally, because direct use improves judgment about what is real and what is marketing.

Regional banks are a cyclical earnings-revision trade, not just a rate trade.

USB and FITB are highlighted because loan growth, deposit pricing, fee trends, and net interest income are moving in the right direction. A steeper yield curve helps, but the real test is whether earnings revisions keep improving into and after results.

Transports confirm the physical-economy side of the AI buildout.

Data centers are physical projects. Servers, power equipment, cooling systems, construction inputs, and replacement parts have to move through the economy. Transport companies can therefore become indirect beneficiaries if demand is strong and industry capacity is disciplined.

Labor data currently supports patience, but inflation is the risk trigger.

Low layoffs and improving hiring are good for the economy. They become a market problem only if they create wage pressure and inflation that force tighter financial conditions. That is why Core PCE remains the main macro check.

Technical calls should become alerts and review flags, not automatic portfolio changes.

Positive KRE/IWM/DE/LLY and negative BTC/MSFT views are useful because they tell us where to look. They do not replace live chart checks, valuation, existing exposure, or thesis status.
