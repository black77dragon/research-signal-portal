HDetailed Appendix For Understanding

The source is short, so this appendix preserves the actual mechanism claims rather than stretching them into a trade recommendation.

Why the slowdown is not automatically bearish.

Jordi's macro analogy is a mid-cycle slowdown: growth remains positive and the expansion is intact, but the rate of change cools. After a parabolic AI repricing, this is normal. The market can repair through time and volatility, not only through a crash.

Why rate-of-change matters.

The AI thematic portfolio's 30-day rate of change peaked in mid-May and has moved back toward zero. That is the actual reset he is pointing to. The price action feels scary because volatility is high, but his point is that rate-of-change has already absorbed a lot of the damage.

Why SK Hynix is a warning but not proof.

The SK Hynix outside day can signal consolidation in memory. But Jordi says it becomes thesis-breaking only if earnings, memory demand, data-center buildout, or capex actually deteriorate. Until then, the correction is part of the mid-cycle slowdown.

Why token usage beats token price as the AI demand signal.

Token prices can fall because the commodity price of inference declines, cheaper models get used, or expensive subscriptions slow during vacation periods. That is not the same as demand failure. For Jordi, the important metric is whether token usage and per-task AI usage keep growing.

Why earnings revisions are the real break trigger.

Jordi ties the AI move to earnings, not retail speculation. Forward EPS and revisions have risen sharply across the S&P, small caps, and global equities. If the market is going to break, the first serious evidence would be earnings estimates rolling over.

Why Apple is both negative and useful.

He says he is negative on Apple and most of the Mag 7 except Tesla, but he still treats Apple's Mac mini / Mac Studio supply constraint as evidence that AI-related memory and compute bottlenecks are not solved. Apple the stock and Apple the supply-chain indicator should be separated.

What loops mean in plain English.

Agents make individual tasks faster. Loops make recurring workflows run repeatedly with less supervision. If loops become common, token demand scales with recurring business processes, not just the number of people using chatbots.

Why loops matter for jobs and margins.

Jordi says loops are where productivity becomes non-linear. They can lift margins, but they also create the labor-displacement pressure because power users can train agents to repeat office workflows around the clock.

Why Q2 matters.

Jordi expects the earnings-season bar to be higher and reactions more negative than Q1 on a rate-of-change basis. That makes Q2 a monitoring window for whether AI winners still beat the higher expectations.
