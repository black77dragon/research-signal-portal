D. Content

Transports ex airlines are recovering after a major relative drawdown.

22V says transport stocks excluding airlines had a record drawdown versus the S&P 1500 and are now rallying alongside other non-AI cyclicals such as banks and retailers.

The recovery is driven by both demand and supply normalization.

The source points to improving trucking demand, stronger global container demand, rising freight rates excluding fuel surcharges, and fading trucking oversupply after the COVID-era surge in small private truckers.

AI buildout is a second-order transport tailwind.

22V says many transport companies mentioned AI as a tailwind in Q1 earnings. The logic is simple: building AI factories requires moving equipment, materials, power/cooling components, and data-center supply-chain inputs.

Margin sentiment is the key company screen.

Forward earnings, sales, and margin estimates are improving. 22V highlights that margin compression had been a major headwind and that transports with better forward-looking margin sentiment are the starting point for individual long research.

Inflation remains the main risk.

If core PCE inflation is too hot over the summer, financial conditions could tighten. That would hurt transports because they are cyclical and risk-on exposed.
