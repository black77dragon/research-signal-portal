The report says the old baseline for investing was unusually friendly.

Alden's starting point is that the late-20th-century investing playbook grew up during a period with expanding equity ownership, index funds, retirement accounts, discount brokerage, globalization, and a relatively strong belief in institutional progress. That matters because many portfolio models assume this environment is normal rather than historically specific.

The information environment is part of the macro risk.

The report treats AI-generated video, bots, weak media trust, and algorithmic amplification as a practical market problem. The point is not just social commentary. It affects how quickly false narratives can move, how hard crisis interpretation becomes, and how investors should treat apparent evidence during stress.

The Wild West label means weaker rules, less coordination, and more self-reliance.

The geopolitical section links trade conflict, US-China rivalry, industrial policy, broken treaty trust, drone warfare, and supply-chain fragility. For portfolio work, the important translation is that operational resilience and source-of-truth discipline become more valuable.

The investment answer is not all-in hard assets; it is a broader portfolio structure.

The three-pillar framework keeps productive equities, commodities/producers and hard monies, and cash-equivalents in separate roles. Productive equities serve growth regimes, commodities and hard monies serve inflation or scarcity regimes, and cash-equivalents serve crisis liquidity. Bonds receive less emphasis because long-duration bonds can fail in stagflation.

The bitcoin-plus-cashflows section is conceptually relevant but not immediately actionable.

Orange Juice is presented as a private vehicle designed to own cash-flowing businesses and compound retained earnings into bitcoin. This is useful as a model for how bitcoin treasury strategies may evolve, but the specific opportunity appears private, accredited-investor oriented, and outside immediate public-market implementation.
