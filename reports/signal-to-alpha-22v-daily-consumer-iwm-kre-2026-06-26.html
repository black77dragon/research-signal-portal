The daily note says the broadening trade is still alive.

22V's message is that the economy is not only about AI infrastructure. Consumer spending, retail stocks, small caps, and regional banks are still part of the market-breadth discussion. That matters because it can reduce dependence on mega-cap AI leadership if the data stay supportive.

Consumer strength is the base case, not a recession warning.

The email points to real spending near 2%, nominal spending near 5%, and income growth around 4.0-4.5%. In plain English, households are still spending and income is still growing enough to support demand. Lower-income spending improvement also reduces the chance that the consumer is quietly breaking.

AI productivity is framed as a demand support, not a job-apocalypse shock.

22V argues that expected AI productivity gains can encourage current spending because households and companies expect higher future income and output. This is the opposite of the bearish story where AI immediately destroys confidence through job-loss fears.

The bank and small-cap signal is useful, but should not override prior preference constraints.

Roque's bullish IWM and KRE view supports a broadening tape. However, Rene has previously been cautious about converting regional-bank notes into work items. The practical use here is to track IWM/KRE as breadth and risk-appetite indicators unless Rene wants a direct bank review.

Inflation remains the key constraint on the constructive cyclical case.

Core PCE matching expectations provides relief, but not a full green light. Strong income, spending, and capex can keep demand healthy, but they can also keep inflation sticky. If services inflation stays high, the Fed may not be able to ease financial conditions as much as cyclicals would like.
