HDetailed Appendix For Understanding

This appendix separates the thesis into plain-English building blocks for later deep-dive work.

1. His investment thesis in one sentence

Pompliano wants to own long-duration secular technologies that can compound for a decade, even if they are volatile and periodically hated, because the best asymmetry appears when structurally strong assets fall out of favor.

2. Why he avoids the middle

He argues that large-cap indexes and Mag 7-style businesses have durability, profits, and cash flow, while small or thematic assets can have major upside. The middle lacks both the safety of the giants and the asymmetry of early themes.

3. Why he still likes Mag 7

He frames recent Mag 7 weakness as fear about inflation and AI capex rather than business deterioration. In his view, buying the same businesses after another six months of growth at similar prices is becoming more attractive.

4. His AI capex answer

He acknowledges token-efficiency pressure but says adoption and addressable market growth can more than offset lower token use per customer. He also argues real demand for software, specialized workflows, data centers, power, and chips remains strong enough that overbuild is not a near-term problem.

5. His public AI portfolio shape

The public portfolio he describes is not primarily SaaS or generic AI software. It is physical AI: Tesla for embodied AI, RoboStrategy for private robotics exposure, and Ondas for drones/autonomous defense.

6. His private AI portfolio shape

He says private-market exposure includes software companies such as Replit, Lovable, and micro1. This gives him a full-stack AI posture: software privately, physical AI publicly.

7. His Bitcoin thesis

Bitcoin remains his check against national debt growth, dollar debasement, loose fiscal policy, and monetary manipulation. The difference is that he now expects institutionalized compounding, not repeated early-cycle 10x or 20x moves.

8. How to use this source

Use it as a map of Pomp's worldview and a source of research candidates. Do not treat it as buy/sell instruction. The right next step is candidate diligence on TSLA, BOT, ONDS, and the physical-AI basket.
