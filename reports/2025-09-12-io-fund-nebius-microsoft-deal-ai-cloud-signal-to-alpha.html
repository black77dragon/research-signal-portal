Chart Pack

No chart pack was copied into this A22 report. The source is an authenticated I/O Fund report; local source text/HTML/PDF are preserved in the I/O Fund archive, but this Signal-to-Alpha output uses summarized evidence rather than republishing source charts.

The Microsoft deal is the validation event.

The report's core point is that Microsoft contracting with Nebius validates a real shortage of AI cloud capacity. The deal matters because hyperscalers normally prefer to own their infrastructure, so outside capacity demand signals a genuine bottleneck.

Delivery matters more than headline contract value.

The contract only becomes financial value if Nebius delivers capacity on time. This makes data-center construction, power availability, GPU delivery, and operational uptime the core milestones to monitor.

Nebius looks less debt-stressed than CoreWeave, but still capital hungry.

The source contrasts Nebius' low-coupon convertible financing with CoreWeave's higher-cost debt. That is a real advantage, but the business still needs large sums for data centers and GPUs, so dilution and future raises remain central watch items.

Vertical integration is the quality upside.

Nebius may have a cost edge through owned data centers, custom server design, low power overhead, and a full-stack AI cloud platform. The investment question is whether these features produce durable margin advantage at scale rather than simply lower prices for customer acquisition.

Subsidiaries add optionality and complexity.

Avride, TripleTen, Toloka, and ClickHouse create optional value paths, but they also make the company harder to analyze. ClickHouse may be a useful capital source; Avride and other projects can also distract from the main AI infrastructure buildout.

The source's own posture is risk-controlled.

The report does not deny upside. It says the risk/reward is not clean enough to chase without better downside protection. That makes the right route watchlist and comparison work, not a buy action.
