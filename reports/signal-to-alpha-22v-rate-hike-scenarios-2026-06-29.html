HDetailed Appendix For Understanding

This appendix is based on the email body. Linked web/PDF content and inline image attachments were not opened.

The delayed hike scenario lets markets keep breathing.

If inflation is only a bit too hot, markets may assume the Fed can wait until December 2026 or Q1 2027. In that case, financial conditions do not tighten much before year-end and cyclicals can still work.

The September hike scenario is the real downside trigger.

If June and July inflation make the Fed forecast obviously too low, markets may price a September hike. That is the path where 2-year yields break higher and risk assets suffer.

The economic speed limit links spending, productivity, and inflation.

22V's speed-limit idea is simple: if productivity is about 2% and labor supply growth is limited, nominal spending around 5% may be too hot. Inflation then stays high unless growth slows or the Fed hikes.

Retail is interesting because sales surprises matter again.

22V argues that retailer revenue surprises are again passing through to earnings surprises after pandemic distortions faded. That makes high-frequency sales data more useful for earnings revisions.

AI is showing up as margin trajectory, not just tech-sector hype.

The note says AI-using retailers have lower margin levels but faster margin improvement. That is useful because it links AI to operational productivity outside pure AI infrastructure.
