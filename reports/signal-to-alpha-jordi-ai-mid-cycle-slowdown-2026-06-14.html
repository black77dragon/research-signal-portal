HDetailed Appendix For Understanding

This appendix is meant to make the argument understandable without watching the video first.

The phrase "mid-cycle slowdown" means the theme is maturing, not dying.

Jordi is saying that the first explosive part of the AI move may be over. That does not mean AI demand disappears. It means investors may stop rewarding every AI-linked exposure equally and begin asking which companies actually earn durable economics.

The most important tension is cheaper AI output versus more expensive AI infrastructure.

If tokens and model access become cheaper, model owners can face margin pressure. At the same time, the physical build-out still needs power, cooling, memory, networking, chips, and specialized materials. This can shift investment opportunity from model providers toward physical suppliers.

The rotation from hyperscalers to broader beneficiaries is the key investment pattern.

The source argues that hyperscalers have lagged while smaller and broader market segments have started to work. In plain English: the market may still like AI, but it is looking for the next group of companies that benefit from the build-out.

Chemicals and materials matter because complexity increases required precision.

As AI hardware becomes more complex, the manufacturing chain often needs more specialized chemicals and materials. This is why Entegris appears in the signal: it may benefit from the less visible but critical material layer of AI infrastructure.
