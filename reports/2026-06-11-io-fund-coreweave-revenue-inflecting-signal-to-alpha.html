Chart Pack

No chart pack was copied into this A22 report. The source is an authenticated I/O Fund report; local source text/HTML/PDF are preserved in the I/O Fund archive, but this Signal-to-Alpha output uses summarized evidence rather than republishing source charts.

The report separates AI demand from stock quality.

The important point is that CoreWeave can have strong demand and still be a difficult stock. Backlog, sold-out capacity, and hyperscaler demand support the scarcity thesis, but the source argues that execution and funding determine whether shareholders benefit.

The revenue setup depends on a steep second-half acceleration.

CoreWeave beat Q1 revenue expectations but guided Q2 below consensus while keeping the full-year outlook. That means the investment case depends on a material acceleration as capacity comes online in the second half of 2026.

Capex is the central tension in the model.

AI infrastructure demand requires large upfront spending on GPUs, power, data-center fit-out, and associated systems. For CoreWeave, the report frames this as a constant need to fund the buildout before the revenue and profit inflection are fully visible.

Debt and interest expense can overwhelm the growth story.

The report's practical warning is that debt-funded growth becomes fragile when interest expense rises faster than revenue quality. Lower rates or easier credit conditions could help the stock, but they do not remove the need to prove durable free cash flow.

Leasing helps but does not remove execution risk.

Leased power and data-center arrangements can reduce construction burden and preserve capital for GPUs. The trade-off is dependence on counterparties for powered-shell delivery, weather, construction, timing, and operational execution.

Software upside remains a test, not a conclusion.

CoreWeave is trying to offer more full-stack and flexible cloud services. That could improve the model over time, but the report is cautious because the largest current customers are hyperscalers that may not need those software add-ons at the same margin profile.
