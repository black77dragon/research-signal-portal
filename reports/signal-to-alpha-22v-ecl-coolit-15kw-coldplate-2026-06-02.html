Source excerpt summary.

Yesterday, CoolIT announced the validation of a 15kW cold plate that as they noted extends the direct to chip single phase roadmap past 2030. (CoolIT Systems Demonstrates 15kW Coldplate).CoolIT had previously announced a 4 kW cold plate design in March of 2025 and has now disclosed a validated 15 kW design, representing the capability of a single cold-plate architecture to remove up to 15 kW of heat under specified operating conditions. It is somewhat difficult to compare but directionally this is indicating a powerful step up in single phase liquid cooling. For context, a competitor review of a GB300 NVL72 rack analysis implies approximately 6.2 kW of compute power per tray distributed across six primary CPU/GPU cold plates, suggesting an average thermal load of roughly 1 kW per cold plate.
