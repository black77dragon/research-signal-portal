This appendix is written so each paragraph heading summarizes the key idea. It is based on the email body; linked PDFs and inline images were not opened.

Concentration is not a short signal by itself.

22V explicitly separates narrow leadership from a timing call. The market can stay concentrated and still rise. The danger begins when concentration meets a macro shock, because the same winners become crowded sources of downside.

The 2-year yield is the cleanest daily trigger.

The 2-year Treasury yield is a simple proxy for Fed policy expectations. If it rises above 4.2% and heads toward 4.3%-4.5%, investors may conclude that hawkish data can force more tightening, which would pressure expensive or crowded winners.

Regional banks benefit if the rate path improves lending economics.

The bank section says higher yields in the 2-5 year part of the curve can help lending economics and net interest income. USB is the named single-stock preference, while KRE is the ETF expression.

CNY weakness is a macro pressure signal rather than a standalone trade.

The CNY section argues that weak domestic growth, PBOC easing, potential Fed hikes, and capital controls point toward a weaker Chinese currency. That matters for global risk and China exposure, but it needs FX-market confirmation.

BTC weakness and Clarity Act delay both reduce crypto urgency.

The Daily email says the Clarity Act is unlikely to pass this session and Roque sees downside risk in BTC. That supports watch mode unless live market momentum improves.
