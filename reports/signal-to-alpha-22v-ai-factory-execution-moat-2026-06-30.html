D. Content

Compute demand still outruns supply, so the AI capex cycle is not obviously pausing.

22V says conversations with operators and developers showed no sign of hyperscalers or AI builders pausing spend. The constraint is still getting usable compute online, not a lack of demand.

Execution is becoming the moat because AI factories are hard to operate.

Bringing large GPU or TPU clusters online requires power delivery, load management, thermal management, chip orchestration, and utilization discipline. If even a modest portion of a large cluster is underutilized, returns can be materially damaged.

Pricing power accrues to capacity plus platform quality.

The note says long-term compute contracts may price above early neocloud deals. It also points to AWS capacity-block price increases as evidence that reliable capacity and strong platforms can command a premium.

Liquid cooling, power, and edge deployments remain important derivative beneficiaries.

The source reinforces liquid cooling, modular power, behind-the-meter generation, and smaller edge deployments as relevant because density, latency, interconnection delays, and community resistance all shape build-out economics.

H. Appendix

Compute scarcity is still the central claim.

The note says demand for compute remains strong and that supply, not appetite, is the binding constraint. This supports the continuing AI infrastructure thesis.

The execution layer may separate winners from hardware collectors.

Owning GPUs is not the same as operating an AI factory. The moat may sit in reliable delivery, power/cooling integration, platform quality, and cluster utilization.

GPU pricing signals are still immature.

22V cautions that token and GPU price discovery is early, spot data is thin, and H100 rental volatility should not be overinterpreted as a clean signal for the whole AI trade.

Orbital data centers remain a watch item, not a base case.

Operators are skeptical because at-scale compute requires frequent physical intervention. That makes orbital maintenance risk a major underwriting problem.
